The Mental Health Issue

By Jake Eyles — May 22, 2025

What is the issue?

Mental health disorders are becoming much more prevalent in recent years, especially anxiety disorders, which can lead to further mental health issues if left untreated. Diagnosis rates started to rise notably in the 1980s, with a significant increase around 2007. Countries like Portugal, Iran, and Lebanon are among the most affected, followed by Australia, Western Europe, and the Americas. Despite efforts, the issue continues to worsen, with approximately 17% of Australians diagnosed with an anxiety disorder. The aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic has further exacerbated mental health challenges worldwide.

What are the effects?

Short-term: Mental health issues can cause distress, behavioral changes, an increased risk of substance abuse, and higher chances of self-harm.

Long-term: Untreated conditions may lead to chronic health issues, social isolation, substance dependence, and difficulty in maintaining education or employment. These effects can last for years or even a lifetime without intervention.

What is the solution?

Solutions include public education for both students and adults to better recognize mental health issues. Making therapy and counseling more affordable—especially in countries like the U.S. where insurance is often a barrier—is also vital. Managing the influence of social media and encouraging healthy portrayals of mental health can make a difference. Government funding would help implement and expand community-based programs.

Why?

Mental health issues are personal and often affect those closest to us. I have experienced them myself, and through this page, I hope to raise awareness and encourage others to support one another.
